From our founder  

Jannah Circle is as deeply personal and as equally precious as my own family. It was seeded in the support, kindness, and sisterhood I found in my community and nurtured by the faith we all shared. Jannah Circle is named after the stillbirth of my child named Jannah.

In the face of our similar struggles, we cultivated a resilient, compassionate, and hopeful community. We are women, children and youth. Young and old. We are parents, students, professionals, entrepreneurs, teachers, advocates, workers, volunteers, but most of all, we are companions together on this path. We seek to benefit and support each other as we strive to bring support to others in the community.

Fariha Rub,MD
Hospital Medicine Physician

Mission

To develop American communities with sacred values through programs that revive, connect, grow, and transform all.

Vision

Jannah Circle works to transform the culture of how diverse families and individuals of all ages are supported in a community. Programs and resources for their physical well being, parenting, socialization, mental health, access to expert education, and self-development workshops. Our purpose is to inspire and mobilize a community that can then be empowered to help even more causes and create long-lasting social change.

American Muslims are struggling. 15-25% report anxiety disorders, 9-30% report mood disorders, and over 50% report one or more major problems with their marriage (The FYI).

Our Muslim community organization has been tirelessly working to provide essential support and services to mothers and children since 2018, through spiritual and social events and experiences.
