Ramadan 2022

The Holy Month of Ramadan is a special time for Muslims around the world; a time of faith and devotion, but also giving and compassion.

The holiest month in the Islamic calendar

Ramadan is an opportunity for spiritual growth, to see loved ones and to help those in need. To commemorate this period, Muslims fast throughout the month as well as praying, reciting the Quran and giving to charity to strengthen their relationship with Allah SWT.

As a faith-based charity, Charity Right fully understands that charitable donations are at the heart of Ramadan. By donating to Charity Right, you can ensure that your Sadaqah during Ramadan will go to those most in need.

What is Ramadan?

As Ramadan 2022 approaches, it’s important to gain a deeper understanding of Islam and gain closeness to Allah SWT. Ramadan takes place during the ninth month of the Hijri calendar. It’s during this holy month that Allah SWT revealed the Quran to the Prophet Muhammad PBUH.

Throughout the month, Muslims make a concerted effort to practice more good deeds and overcome any negative traits they may have.

Fasting is mandatory during Ramadan for those who are healthy and able to fast, eating only a small amount of food to provide fuel for the day – suhoor - before sunrise and iftar after sunset.

Muslims also abstain from immoral behaviour or any impure and unkind thoughts. By fasting, praying and observing the holy month, we gain closeness to Allah SWT, our sings are forgiven, and we end Ramadan as better people than we were when we entered.

Zakat is one of the five pillars of Islam. It’s every Muslims duty to donate money to charity, particularly during a holy time like Ramadan. That’s why it’s so important to find a Ramadan charity, to fulfil this important obligation.

During the month, Muslims are required to give fitrana as a charitable donation (also known as Zakat -ul-Fitr). At the time of the Prophet, fitrana was a ‘saa’, equivalent to four ‘madds’ – i.e. the amount of food you can scoop with both hands. Translated into today’s money, fitrana is worth about £5. Any Muslim who has more food than they need must pay Zakat -ul-Fitr (fitrana).

Giving in Ramadan is particularly important in the final 10 days leading up to Eid-ul-Fitr, as it is during these last ten days that the first verse of the Holy Quran was revealed to the Prophet Muhammad PBUH. Any charitable act or donation will result in increased blessings and rewards from Allah SWT.
