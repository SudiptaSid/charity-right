What is Zakat Al-Fitr?

Zakat Al-Fitr, or the Zakat of Breaking the Fast of Ramadan, is an obligatory special alms payment made by Muslims at the end of Ramadan.

It is also known as Sadaqat Al-Fitr, the Charity of Breaking the Fast, and Zakat Al-Fitrah, Alms from Human Nature, or the Alms of the Human Creation, because it is a mandatory charity every Muslim is obligated to pay at the end of Ramadan, regardless of age or gender.

The corresponding obligation is called fitrah, which is derived from fitr, which represents the "nature" from which God created the human being, and is also the Arabic word for break of fast (iftar), meaning eating after abstaining from it for a period.

What is the Spiritual Function of Zakat Al-Fitr?

As a form of worship, fasting is, if you will, the "spiritual technology" that God has taught man. Its purpose is to free someone's soul from the worldly appetites that preoccupy it for a short time. This will allow the heavenly nature to be freed from the confines of clay so that it can be closer to God.

In this way, the perfection of fasting, the worship rite, demands more of one than merely depriving the body of drink, food and passionate fulfillment. A mind that masters its physical nature will be able to master the human spirit. So fasting also requires us to hold our tongues from unseemly speech.

This is fasting in its purest form, a state no human being can attain. Zakaat Al-Fitr, the obligatory alms given at the end of Ramadan's month-long fast cleanses our fast of the adulterations accumulated during its observation.

What is the amount of Zakat Al-Fitr?

Zakat Al-Fitr is only $13 per person. It is not meant to be a hefty amount that is unaffordable. However, when each person from the community comes together, it can make a huge difference in the lives of the impoverished.
