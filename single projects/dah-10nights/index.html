Plan your Ramadan 2022 Donations for the Last Ten Nights

Dar Al-Hijrah wants to ensure that you have the opportunity to continue giving your Zakat or Sadaqah and not miss out on Laylut al Qadr. The responsibilities of daily life keep us occupied, but by automating giving for the last ten nights, we can simplify giving back to the community.

During the last 10 nights of Ramadan, we are meant to memorize the Qur'an, reflect on its meaning, and do some self-reflection. There is much to do during these final days and nights, including making sure our neighbors don't go to bed hungry, thirsty, or without secure prospects for the future.

How Does it Work?

Giving charity on Laylat-al-Qadr (The Night of Power) has been compared to a thousand months of giving (Al-Qadr, 97:3). During this Holy Night, it is believed that the first verses of the Qur'an were revealed to Prophet Muhammad (PBUH). While it is not known what night forms the Night of Power, it is one of the last 10 nights of Ramadan.

Don’t miss out on reaping the rewards of Laylut al Qadr, schedule your donations for the last ten nights.
