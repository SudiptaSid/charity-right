Why Support CAIR-NY?

Meeting the Communities Request for Legal Assistance

Every year, CAIR-NY receives hundreds of legal requests ranging from immigration to discrimination to law enforcement issues.

The Legal Department's role is to defend and empower Muslim New Yorkers, and CAIR-NY prioritizes each request individually and thoroughly. Additionally, we inform members of our communities about their rights in different settings, such as interactions with law enforcement, travel rights, and workplace discrimination.

You can help us meet these demands, enabling us with the resources to empower those without a voice, and those whose voices go unheard.

Championing Rights for Our Women and Children

We have provided advocacy and representation to our brothers and sisters in several unfortunate instances.

A Muslim woman was arrested and was forced to remove her hijab in Yonkers Police Department custody for over 36 hours. CAIR-NY is calling for an end to the discriminatory practice of forcing suspects to remove hijabs and other religious attire when in custody.

A school employee forced a nine-year-old fasting Muslim student to watch all his classmates eat while fasting after punishing him for allegedly laughing during a lunch break. CAIR-NY acted and filed a civil rights complaint against the NYCDOE for failing to respond to the student's faith-based administrative bullying.

Muslim-led civil rights organization

We consistently work towards ensuring Muslims stay strong and empowered in America.

For over 23 years, CAIR-NY has remained the only Muslim-led civil rights organization in New York. To ensure the civil liberties for Muslim Americans, we promote community activism and civic engagement, we advocate for fair legislation and policies, and we educate New Yorkers through a variety of workshops and publications. In our efforts to amplify the voices of our community members, we will continue to relentlessly advocate for them until justice and equality is rendered. For us, every case is personal, which is why it is our duty to protect and uplift our community.
