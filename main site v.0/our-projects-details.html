The issue

Around the world, millions of children are trapped in poverty.

This is because of a lack of nutrition, inadequate sanitation and poor health and wellbeing that affects life chances.

In addition, these children are often excluded from education or can’t go to school because they need to work or do domestic chores at home.

The solution

Meals in schools can start to transform lives forever

By providing school meals, we offer parents and guardians a huge incentive to send their children to school.

As a result, these children are much more likely to stay healthy, get a life-changing education and have fun with friends.

Our work in

Bangladesh

In Bangladesh, millions of children suffer from malnutrition that prevents them from reaching all of their mental and physical potential.

To change this situation, we provide meals every day to hundreds of children in Dhaka's slums and Rohingya refugees in the city of Cox’s Bazaar.

Our work in

Pakistan

Death from starvation is a constant threat to children who live in the Thar desert, one of the harshest environments on earth.

To keep children safe and help them get a life-changing education, we cook fresh food in schools every day for around 1,500 children.

Our work in

Sudan

Around 14,000 Eritrean refugees live in the Um Gargour camp in Sudan. For most children there, food is scarce and the chance of getting an education is remote.

This is why the meals we provide at Um Gargour are vital to improving the health and futures of these children.
